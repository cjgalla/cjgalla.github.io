Project Overview

Purpose:This photography website is designed to showcase the travel photography collection of my dad, who has journeyed across nearly all U.S. states and some international destinations. The goal of this site is to not only display the beauty of nature through high-quality images, but also to inspire others to explore the outdoors and discover new places to visit and experience the natural wonders of the world.

Intended Users: Intended for travel enthusiasts, nature lovers, and anyone who might have been to other places that they want to share with others.

Content Overview: The website will have a homepage featuring a few of the favorite pictures of my Dads, and an overview of the website and why he chose this concept. It will also have an about page which is going to go into about my dad and his travels, and talk about his love for nature and the world around us. Then there will be two gallery pages, the first is an all United States gallery featuring pictures from his and our cross country trips accross America. The second is an international gallery featuring pictures from the few places outside of the country, and why traveling can be so beneficial for you as a person. Then lastly there will be a review form for any comments or recommendations for future trips.

Page Design

Home

  • Purpose: To welcome users and introduce the photography website. It will showcase a preview of featured photos and explain the intent behind the site.
  • Audience: Photography lovers, travelers, family and friends, and individuals curious about nature and adventure photography.
  • Content: A brief introduction to the site, a few highlighted images, and an overview of the purpose and structure of the website.
  • Data Fields: No user input fields on this page.
  • Buttons: Navigation buttons to explore the other pages.
  • Actions: Clicking the navigation buttons will take the user to other pages such as the about section or the galleries.

About

  • Purpose: To share the story behind the photography project — how the client (my dad) developed his passion for photography, and the family’s journey to nearly every U.S. state.
  • Audience: Visitors who want to learn more about the background of the photos and the photographer's experience.
  • Content: Background story, travel experiences, photography philosophy, and a deeper look at the inspiration behind the photos.
  • Data Fields: None.
  • Buttons or hyperlinks: Button or link to navigate to the galleries or the review form.
  • Actions: Clicking links navigates the user to other pages of the site.

United States Gallery

  • Purpose: Display photographs taken across the United States, organized by region or state to highlight natural diversity.
  • Audience: Users who enjoy nature, travel photography, or are curious about different parts of the U.S.
  • Content: Grid or carousel-style layout of images with optional captions or location tags.
  • Data Fields: None.
  • Buttons: Navigation arrows for gallery scrolling, filter buttons for regions (optional).
  • Actions: Clicking a photo may enlarge it in a lightbox; filters change what is displayed; buttons navigate the gallery.

International Gallery

  • Purpose: Share photos taken outside the U.S., showcasing landscapes, landmarks, and culture from international trips.
  • Audience: Photography fans and travelers interested in scenic and cultural exploration from around the world.
  • Content: Another gallery layout similar to the U.S. page, with international photo collections.
  • Data Fields: None.
  • Buttons: Navigation arrows for scrolling through the photos.
  • Actions: Clicking a photo enlarges it; navigation arrows move through image sets.

Review Form

  • Purpose: Allow users to leave feedback, comments, and suggestions, including ideas for future photography locations.
  • Audience: Anyone who visits the site and wants to contribute thoughts, questions, or place recommendations.
  • Content: A simple contact form asking for name, email, message, and a suggestion field for destinations.
  • Data Fields: Fields for name, email, message, and location recommendation.
  • Validation: Email field should be properly formatted, required fields must be filled out.
  • Buttons: Submit button for the form.
  • Actions: Submitting the form will show a thank-you message and log the input (or send via email/service depending on implementation).

Dynamic Functionality

The website will include several interactive features powered by JavaScript and jQuery. The gallery pages will use lightbox pop-ups to enlarge images and may offer filtering by location or category. The review form will validate user input and provide feedback on successful submission.

Additional features will include a collapsible navigation menu for mobile users, hover effects on images, and a well designed gallery page to allow maximum user engagement. These dynamic touches aim to make the site feel smooth, engaging, and professional.
